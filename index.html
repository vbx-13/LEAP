Project Overview

LEAP (Limitless Exploration/Advanced Pace) is a 9,000-acre innovation district in Boone County, Indiana. Spearheaded by the Indiana Economic Development Corporation (IEDC), it aims to create a high-tech ecosystem for life sciences, microelectronics, clean tech, and EV industries.

Sustainability and Design

Eco-corridors

Wolf, Deer, and Prairie Creeks support biodiversity and green travel paths.

Green Infrastructure

Includes solar panels, native landscaping, and low light pollution.

Smart Water Systems

Features wetlands and vegetated swales for responsible water management.

Major Investments

Eli Lilly has committed over $13 billion for manufacturing facilities. Meta plans a $4.8 billion data center with cutting-edge infrastructure needs.

Economic and Environmental Impact

LEAP is projected to create over 50,000 jobs. State investments approach $1 billion. Environmental considerations around water use and resource sustainability remain a focus of public discussion.

Location & Connectivity

Located off I-65 northwest of Lebanon, Indiana, LEAP offers ideal proximity to Indianapolis and Purdue University, enhancing logistics, workforce access, and research synergy.

Proud Partner

The LEAP Innovation District is proud to partner with leading Indiana companies like F.C. Tucker Company, contributing their expertise to the growth and development of this transformative project.
